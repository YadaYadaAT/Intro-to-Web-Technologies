Mantidae
This is the largest family of praying mantises and includes many common species. Mantids in this family are often referred to as "mantids" or "mantises." Examples include the European mantis (Mantis religiosa) and the Carolina mantis (Stagmomantis carolina).
Hymenopodidae
This family includes mantises with distinctive spiny forelegs. The orchid mantis (Hymenopus coronatus) is a notable member of this family.
Empusidae
Mantises in this family are often known as coneheads due to their elongated and cone-shaped heads. The conehead mantis (Empusa pennata) is an example.
Liturgusidae
This family is found mainly in the Americas. The Amazonian mantis (Liturgusa algorei) is a well-known species in this family.
Deroplatyidae
Also known as the dead leaf mantises, these insects resemble dried leaves. An example is the dead leaf mantis (Deroplatys desiccata).
Phyllocranidae
Members of this family are commonly referred to as leaf mantises due to their leaf-like appearance. The Indian flower mantis (Creobroter gemmatus) is one such species.
Gonypetidae
This family includes mantises with flattened bodies. The spiny flower mantis (Pseudocreobotra wahlbergi) is an example.
